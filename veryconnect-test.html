Who We Are

In response to the changing landscape of Afghanistan, many women professionals and activists sought refuge worldwide. Despite their displacement, their commitment to advocacy remained steadfast. Recognizing the need for a centralized platform, DROPS created Paiwast (which means "Connect" in Pashto and Dari), a digital hub for Afghan women to connect, share ideas, and collaborate. Paiwast symbolizes our dedication to empowering Afghan women, providing a space for education, empowerment, and unity.

Established in 2014, DROPS is a policy-oriented research institute conducting evidence-based research with a focus on empowering women and girls.

What We Do

Powered by DROPS, Paiwast showcases and archives the important work of Afghan women in peace and stability efforts. It serves as a hub for information, connecting organizations and individuals working towards gender equality and social justice. Users can access diverse resources, including research articles, publications, videos, and event announcements. Paiwast also features forums and discussion groups for dialogue and strategy, fostering a sense of community and solidarity.

Join us at Paiwast and be part of the movement towards a more peaceful, equal, and just society for all Afghan women.

What We Offer

Literature Archive

A dedicated section providing access to a wide range of literary works produced by Afghan women, including novels, essays, research papers, and more.

Civil Society Organization Directory

A comprehensive directory of civil society organizations focused on women's empowerment, peacebuilding, and stability in Afghanistan. Users can search and connect with these organizations for collaboration or support.

Discussion Forums

Interactive forums and discussion boards where users can engage in conversations, share ideas, and exchange knowledge related to the Afghan women's movement for peace and stability.

User Profiles and Networking

Users can create personalized profiles to showcase their expertise, connect with other users, and collaborate on projects or initiatives.

Resource Center

A curated collection of reports, videos, webinars, and other educational materials to provide users with valuable information and resources.
