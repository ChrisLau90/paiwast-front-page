Who We Are

At the Organization for Policy Research and Development Studies (DROPS) we firmly believe that a strong policy-oriented research institute is vital for creating healthy environments in states undergoing transition from war-to-peace. With this in mind, DROPS was founded in 2014 in Kabul, Afghanistan, and established a branch in Canada in 2021, as an interdisciplinary and independent research-oriented non-governmental organization. Committed to strengthening the values of inclusivity and pluralism in Afghanistan, DROPS conducts evidence-based research that provide policymakers with alternative solutions to national issues.

In keeping with this belief, we argue that it is only through indigenous research that is bottom-up, locally rooted and representative of all voices in society, particularly women and girls, can intellectual spaces be nourished to incite policy dialogue, public-private partnerships, and good governance. Therefore, through its gender-segregated data, policy analyses, and specialized trainings in critical thinking and research methodology, DROPS ensures that citizens remain at the center of all developmental efforts while enabling them to become the agents of change they want to see.

Paiwast is an initiative of DROPS with the aim of providing a platform for Afghan women to share their knowledge and experiences. Through Paiwast, women can connect, learn from each other, and support one another in their personal and professional journeys. The hub serves as a space for empowerment, education, and collaboration, helping to elevate the voices and contributions of Afghan women in society.

What We Do

At Paiwast/Connect, we are dedicated to showcasing and archiving the rich literature, resources, and initiatives that highlight the important work being done by Afghan women in the pursuit of peace and stability in their country.

Paiwast serves as a centralized hub for information, connecting organizations, forums, and individuals who are working towards gender equality, social justice, and sustainable peace in Afghanistan.

Through our digital platform, users can access a diverse range of resources, including research articles, publications, videos, and event announcements, all focused on amplifying the voices and experiences of Afghan women. We also provide a space for civil society organizations to network, collaborate, and share best practices, fostering a sense of community and solidarity within the women’s movement in Afghanistan.

Additionally, our portal features interactive forums and discussion groups where individuals can engage in dialogue, exchange ideas, and strategize on how to effectively advocate for women’s rights and empowerment. By promoting knowledge-sharing and collaboration, we aim to empower Afghan women and their allies to create positive change in their communities and beyond.

Join us at Paiwast and be a part of the movement towards a more peaceful, equal, and just society for all Afghan women.

What We Offer

Literature Archive

A dedicated section providing access to a wide range of literary works produced by Afghan women, including novels, essays, research papers, and more.

Civil Society Organization Directory

A comprehensive directory of civil society organizations focused on women's empowerment, peacebuilding, and stability in Afghanistan. Users can search and connect with these organizations for collaboration or support.

Discussion Forums

Interactive forums and discussion boards where users can engage in conversations, share ideas, and exchange knowledge related to the Afghan women's movement for peace and stability.

User Profiles and Networking

Users can create personalized profiles to showcase their expertise, connect with other users, and collaborate on projects or initiatives.

Resource Center

A curated collection of reports, videos, webinars, and other educational materials to provide users with valuable information and resources.
